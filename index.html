About me

Research

My work spans immunology, vaccine development, and biosecurity. Below is an overview of projects past and present.

Mirror bacteria & MBDF

I joined the Mirror Biology Dialogues Fund in 2024 as a Senior Scientist, where I research the feasibility, risks, and governance options for mirror bacteria: synthetic organisms constructed from mirror-image biomolecules. I helped co-author the Technical Report on Mirror Bacteria, a 300 page report assessing their feasibility, environmental, and immunological risks. My work also examines how emerging technologies may accelerate key capabilities in this space, and I regularly engage with scientists, biosecurity experts, and policymakers to assess risks and mitigation strategies. Vaccines & Global Health

At 1Day Sooner, I led a team of researchers to push for accelerating the rollout of the newly approved R21 malaria vaccine in Africa, conducting cost-efficacy analyses and regulatory advocacy. Before that, as a Senior Scientist at Alvea, I helped design and test three vaccine candidates in preclinical trials: a shelf-stable DNA vaccine against Omicron BA.2, an 18-valent inhalable SARS-CoV-2 vaccine, and a 25-valent pan-influenza A vaccine. I also co-authored a biosecurity report with the Council on Strategic Risks and have written about vaccine science for Asimov Press.

Immunology research

I completed my Ph.D. in Immunology at the University of Chicago in 2021, advised by Dr. Erin Adams. My dissertation examined the molecular basis of self-antigen recognition by regulatory T cells, asking how the immune system distinguishes self from non-self in ways that govern cancer immunity and autoimmune disease. I applied protein engineering, next-generation sequencing, and X-ray crystallography techniques to answer fundamental questions in T cell biology.
