Top artists, all-time

I love each of these artists. I've seen 14 of them live (not counting the ripper Led Zeppelin cover band I saw in high school, but yes counting Bach performances by other musicians). I don't have room to write about them all, so I'll just say that the top spot is earned by Sigh, an avante-garde black metal band from Japan fronted by a PhD physicist and saxophonist named Dr. Mikannibal. They're surprisingly diverse discography across 13 albums is great for listening while working, exercising, or actively listening. They're not for everyone, however.

Genres over time (coarse)

Highlights:

2015-2017 were my first winters in Chicago, and I listened to a lot of wintry black metal music to get me through them.

But in 2018, my fourth winter, I started desparately missing the California sun, so I listened to an absurd amount of (not very good) reggae music instead. This permanently changing my listening habits and (somewhat better) reggae has persisted in my rotation ever since. Winter does bad things to people.

In 2021 I was road tripping for 3+ months (it was great), and metal is not very pleasant to listen to in my car, so it dipped lower than it had before or sense.

Post-2022: Metal, classical, and ambient are great to listen to while working, so they persist as the top three coarse genres.

New artists each year

In 2025 I switched from mostly listening on Spotify to a self-hosted media server and my personal .mp3 collection of about 24,000 songs. This means I listen to less new music, and re-listen to albums more often, which, so far, is something I'm enjoying. I found with Spotify I was always listening to "new" music such that I often couldn't remember or capture the new stuff I liked. 1000 new artists a year is 3 new artists every day. That's too many. I still crave new music and seek it out, but it's a much more deliberate process now.

What time of day do I listen?

The habit is clear: meetings in the morning, then a dip for lunch, then music to help power through the afternoon and straight on into the evening for wind-down.

Though I know that some of the Japanese artists and possibly others in the dataset were more difficult to find metadata for so they may be underrepresented.

Pareto: how concentrated are my listens?

80% of my listening comes from 44% of the songs I've listened to the last 13 years. That's ~15,000 songs, which sounds about right from my 11,000 song library on Spotify.
